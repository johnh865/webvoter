WebVoter

By John Huang

Webvoter is a simple online tool to create polls and elections using a variety of traditional and experimental voting methods. In additional to the traditional "first-past-the-post", or plurality, voting method, Webvoter includes:

Ranked Ballot Methods

Ranked methods rank candidates in order of most to least preferred. A sample ballot for example is shown below:

Ranked methods include:

  • Instant runoff voting (IRV)
  • Top-two Automatic Runoff
  • Condorcet-compliant Smith-minimax
  • Condorcet-complaint Ranked Pairs
  • Borda Count

Scored Ballot Methods

Scoerd methods rate candidates from a scale. An example scored ballot is shown below:

Scored methods include:

  • Scored voting
  • Majority judgment
  • Smith score
  • STAR voting (Score then Automatic Runoff)

Proportionate Representation Methods

Several voting methods have been devised in order to construct proportionate representation when selecting multiple winners. These methods include:

  • Single transferable Vote
  • Reweighted Range Voting
  • Sequential Monroe Voting
